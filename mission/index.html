Why this exists

Built for the people
technology forgot.

Ask any AI to build you a daily news page and you'll get a different output every time. This repo pre-bakes the answer — so the people who need it most don't have to fix it.

You can ask AI to build almost anything. Most people don't know that. The ones who do, often give up after the third attempt — because every output looks different, the images break, the layout is from 2003, and the sections they care about are missing.

This project starts from the other end. Hundreds of small decisions — typography, layout, source preferences, structure, what counts as a story, how it's laid out, how it links back — are already made. Not because they're objectively right, but because somebody already made them, reviewed them, lived with them. That somebody is me. The work is shared.

Who this is for

It's not for developers. They can already do this in their sleep.

It's for the people who hear "AI can build websites now" and don't know that means them too. Your mum. Your uncle. Maybe your grandma. Anyone who looks at a chat box and sees a search engine, not a workshop.

For your mum, your uncle,
maybe your grandma.

The one-step principle

One sentence. The file lands on your disk. You double-click. You read your news.

No installation. No accounts. No keys. No plugins. No git. No subscription tier you have to compare. The free version of whatever AI is in your browser already does this. You just had to know it could.

Why minimal cognitive cost matters

Every step you have to learn is a step where someone gives up. Every time the output looks broken, every "log in to continue", every "now configure your environment" is a door closing on someone who would have used the thing if it had just worked.

Pre-baking removes those doors. The repo isn't fancy code. It's a set of opinions other people can borrow.

A learning project, too

The whole repo is open. Read it. Steal from it. Fork it and change the topics. If you've ever copy-pasted a Markdown file you can make this yours. That's also the point — the pattern (AI assistant + prompt collection + style sheet = polished artifact) works for anything: travel briefs, sports recaps, market digests, your grandfather's favourite hobby.

It's a tool. It's also a worked example.
